The Problem

Great ideas die in the gap
between vision and execution.

01
No Structured Framework
Students and first-time founders lack the product management foundation to translate ideas into buildable specs.
02
Market Blindness
Without competitor analysis and user research, teams build for problems that don't exist or markets that are already saturated.
03
Prioritization Paralysis
Feature lists grow unbounded with no clear framework for what to build first, leading to scope creep and stalled progress.
04
Documentation Debt
PRDs written in silos, updated inconsistently, and disconnected from the actual roadmap create misalignment across teams.
05
Stakeholder Misalignment
Without a shared source of truth, NGOs and startup teams waste cycles in meetings rather than building.
06
Inaccessible PM Tools
Enterprise product management tools are expensive, bloated, and designed for teams of 50 — not scrappy innovators of 3.
